## Possible Overlaps With Requirements Extracted From Other RFCs
- Section 2.1 overview prohibitions on TLS ChangeCipherSpec and TLS KeyUpdate overlap the dedicated QUIC-specific adjustments in RFC 9001 Sections 8.4 and 6.
- Packet-type mappings in RFC 9001 Section 4 Table 1 overlap packet-structure and packet-number-space rules in RFC 9000 Section 17 and the packet protection overview in RFC 9001 Section 5.
- ClientHello padding and packet coalescing behavior in RFC 9001 Section 4.3 overlaps QUIC transport datagram size requirements in RFC 9000 Section 14.1.
- 0-RTT usage restrictions in RFC 9001 Sections 2.1, 4.6, and 5.6 overlap application-specific replay guidance such as HTTP/3 replay handling and RFC 9221 datagram 0-RTT profiling.
- Header field layouts shown in RFC 9001 Section 5.4.1 Figure 7 overlap RFC 9000 packet format definitions even though the protected and sampled regions are TLS-specific here.
- Retry packet field layout material in RFC 9001 Section 5.8 overlaps RFC 9000 Retry packet structure, while the integrity tag algorithm remains specific to RFC 9001.
- KEY_UPDATE_ERROR handling in RFC 9001 Section 6 overlaps transport error handling semantics in RFC 9000, but the key update conditions are specific to RFC 9001.
